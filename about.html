About Bakari

View Full Details

People commonly ask me, "Why the switch from aerospace to electrical engineering?" The short answer is I've always been curious about technology and how it works, down to its core. Technology is multi-disciplinary by nature, and requires knowing more than just mechanics. So it's not really a switch.

The long answer goes back to my professional experience and internships. During those times, I learned that strategic innovation requires an understanding of what is truly limiting a technology's performance. Algorithms and hardware teams generally work in isolation. However, hardware and software are not mutually exclusive; innovation towards new systems that can be fielded tomorrow requires concurrent development with both the hardware and software in mind. My objective is to innovate at the interface of computation and hardware to develop tomorrow's solutions. My applied physics bachelor's helps me identify fundamental physical limitations, my aerospace engineering bachelor's taught me about the complexities of space, my electrical engineering master's exposed me to a breadth of modern signal processing methods, and my computational imaging doctoral program is where I am developing technical depth at the hardware-software-physics interface.
